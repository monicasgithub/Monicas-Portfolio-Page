🐾 Monica's Portfolio Proposal

Strategic Plan and File Architecture

Website Purpose

This is a personal portfolio website built to demonstrate my HTML, CSS, and JavaScript skills developed over the course of this semester. The site is designed around an original pastel color palette and custom cat mascots to give the project a consistent and memorable visual identity while remaining simple enough to build entirely with front-end technologies (no back-end or server-side processing required).

Beyond fulfilling the requirements of this course, the long-term purpose of the site is to function as a professional showcase that can be shared with potential employers, used in networking, and linked from a resume or LinkedIn profile.

Target Audience

  • Primary: hiring managers and recruiters that evaluate candidates skills for frontend development positions
  • Secondary: course instructor, who will evaluate technical execution and code structure
  • Tertiary: peers and other network contacts to share personal brand with

Components of an Individual Portfolio

The essential components that every individual portfolio page needs include:

  • About Me Section: a description of who you are, your education, your experience and other noteworthy information about you.
  • Skills Section: explain what you are capable of and which coding languages you have experience with.
  • Projects Section: curate a list of projects you completed to showcase your work.

What This Portfolio Will Include

  • A clear hero/intro section stating who I am and what role I'm aiming for
  • An About page with background, interests, and personality (including cat branding)
  • A Skills page presenting HTML/CSS/JS (and any other) competencies, demonstrating proficiency by directly including them in the page (e.g. using JS for cute animations)
  • A Projects page with 3–5 curated project
  • A Contact page with an easy-to-find, working contact form
